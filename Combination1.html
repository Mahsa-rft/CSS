Introducing HTML5

HTML: The Next Generation kicked off back in June 2004 and is still being drafted. Indeed, it could be ten years before HTML5 becomes the online standard,although some of its features are already stable enough to be implemented today.

We need it: HTML must be brought up to date if it’s to serve the needs of the modern web, and the move from a document-based internet to a multimedia-rich one. The markup language hasn’t been revamped to any meaningful degree since 2001, when XHTML 1.1 was granted the iconic W3C Recommendation status.
